Scientific resarch in systems security

Mentored by André Grégio (gregio@inf.ufpr.br)

October 2019 - December 2020

At the Federal University of Paraná, my involvement in systems security research culminated in the development of a project named "Breaking Good," which aimed at crafting adversarial malware to test and improve the resilience of static antivirus defenses against evasion techniques. The project was notable for several accomplishments. We engineered malware capable of evading detection by closely mimicking the behavioral patterns of legitimate software, a breakthrough that significantly challenges conventional antivirus methodologies. Our innovative work received recognition through its publication at the Brazilian Security Symposium (SBSeg), marking a significant milestone in our research journey.

Further validating the effectiveness of our approach, we secured a victory in an evasion competition, an achievement that is documented in the publication roots_mlsec20. This victory underscored the practical implications and potential of our research in real-world cybersecurity challenges. In addition to these accolades, we committed to contributing to the broader cybersecurity community by making our findings and methodologies available on GitHub under the repository Breaking good. This open-source sharing initiative aimed at fostering further research and development in the field by providing a base for other researchers to build upon.

The project was a collaborative effort, involving a team of experts whose contributions were instrumental to its success. Details of this collaboration can be found at secret, a platform that highlights the collective expertise and dedication of our team. Through "Breaking Good," we showcased our ability to develop innovative solutions to critical cybersecurity challenges, contributing to the ongoing evolution of more secure systems. For a more comprehensive insight into our research and findings, I invite interested parties to explore our publication on ResearchGate at Breaking good research, which details the methodologies and outcomes of our work.
